🩸 Hematology Consultation Prep

Bring this entire section to the hematology consult. The platelet trajectory (762 → 927 → 1,130 over 24 months) crossed with CRP normalization (192 → <6) is the central diagnostic question. Shela's case sits at the intersection of four evidence bases (HS post-splenectomy, DIE-platelet axis, Vit D megakaryopoiesis, CCM antithrombotics) with no single guideline covering her phenotype.

The One-Paragraph Story

36F Filipino, 16 years post-splenectomy for hereditary spherocytosis (autosomal dominant), post-cholecystectomy 2010 (chronic fat malabsorption), with deep infiltrating endometriosis (torus uterinus obliterated, rectal lesion 16.5×6×20mm), Mirena IUS in situ (amenorrheic, hemodynamically stable), growing left posterior parietal cavernoma (80% volume increase 2020-2025, no documented hemorrhage). Platelets rose 762 → 927 → 1,130 across 24 months despite CRP falling 192 → <6 (32× improvement), thyroid normal first-ever testing, HbA1c 4.30, ESR normalized 32 → 10. Apr 7 smear: normocytic normochromic RBCs, neutrophil-predominant WBC (65%), platelets increased with some microplatelets. Vit D recovering 16.53 → 28.52 ng/mL (D3 5,000 IU + K2 MK-7 started Mar 25). Creatinine 1.17 (above ULN 1.13). BP 92/58.

Three Likely Drivers (literature-supported)

  1. Post-splenectomy baseline — expected 400–600 baseline; explains chronic elevation but NOT ongoing escalation. Schilling J Thromb Haemost 2008 (PMID 18485083): late HS arterial event HR 7.2, venous HR 3.3, events cluster ≥1 decade post-op.
  2. Endometriosis-platelet feedback loop — platelets actively drive endometriotic fibrogenesis via TGF-β/SMAD; advanced DIE shows the EXACT phenotype on Shela's smear (high platelets + microplatelets/low MPV). Avcioğlu 2014 PMC4076665, Guo lab series 2015–2019 (PMID 25740881, 27519725, 27724926).
  3. Chronic Vit D deficiency disinhibiting megakaryopoiesis — VDR expressed on megakaryocytes; D activation inhibits maturation. Shela had ~24 months of severe deficiency exactly overlapping the platelet rise. Silvagno PLoS ONE 2010 PMC2809087, Aihara JBC 2004.

⚠️ TESTS TO SKIP (low yield in Filipino patient)

  • Factor V Leiden — essentially absent in Filipino/Asian populations
  • Prothrombin G20210A — same; near-zero prevalence
  • Source: Ng HJ et al. Singapore Blood 2006 explicitly recommends against routine FVL/PT screening in SE Asian VTE patients

🚫 DO NOT START aspirin until vWF clears

Standard reflex is to start ASA 81mg in extreme thrombocytosis. This is wrong without a VWF workup first. Acquired vWD is documented at platelet counts in her range and aspirin in undiagnosed AvWS causes hemorrhage. After VWF results, the cavernoma question is also less restrictive than traditionally taught: Kuroedov JAHA 2024 (PMID 38471833, n=2,709) found antithrombotics associated with LOWER intracranial hemorrhage in CCM patients (OR 0.21, 95% CI 0.13–0.35). The 80% growth has not been studied as a stratification variable. Neurosurgery should weigh in if Shela's cavernoma has ever bled.

The Mirena Question (settled)

Mirena is NOT driving the platelet count. Published mechanism literature shows LNG-IUS decreases platelets (Maruo PubMed 12057782). Lidegaard Danish cohort (BMJ 2009 PMID 19679613) shows no VTE risk with LNG-IUS. WHO/CDC MEC Category 2 (safe) for thrombosis risk. Removal is not a hematologic intervention. Subtle caveat: years of amenorrhea CAN cause iron retention (PMC3682695) — ferritin will tell us. Removal would only be reconsidered if (a) ferritin is elevated, OR (b) gynecology decides to switch to dienogest/GnRH antagonist for stronger DIE control. Do not stop Mirena without an immediate replacement plan.

3-Month Recheck Strategy

Repeat CBC + ferritin + Vitamin D in late June 2026. If platelets fall after Vit D reaches 50+ ng/mL and ferritin is normalized, the puzzle solves itself non-invasively. If platelets remain >1,000 with all the above tests negative, bone marrow biopsy with reticulin staining is the next step (BSH threshold for mandatory BM biopsy is >1,500, so she has runway).

Symptoms requiring immediate medical attention

  • New unilateral headache, vision changes, weakness/numbness on one side (cavernoma + thrombocytosis)
  • Calf swelling, pain, warmth (DVT)
  • Sudden upper abdominal pain with nausea (portal/mesenteric vein thrombosis)
  • Chest pain or shortness of breath (PE)
  • Fever >38.5°C — needs antibiotics urgently due to functional asplenia (OPSI risk)
  • Unexplained bleeding (gums, nose, easy bruising) — could indicate AvWS
