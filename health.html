Source Hierarchy

Trust Levels (Highest to Lowest)
1
Peer-reviewed research (PubMed, Cochrane)
Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, RCTs
2
Medical organizations (ACOG, ESHRE)
Clinical practice guidelines, consensus statements
3
Specialist organizations (Endo Foundation, Cavernoma Alliance)
Patient education materials backed by medical boards
4
Medical institutions (Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins)
Hospital/university health information pages
5
Individual practitioners
Doctor blogs, specialist opinions (verify credentials)
6
Wellness bloggers, social media, anecdotal
Cross-reference everything. Many well-meaning but unverified claims.

Red Flag Patterns

Warning Signs in Health Content
CURE claims "This cured my endometriosis" — Endo has no cure, only management.
Anti-medication "Throw away your pills" — Dangerous for someone on critical management.
Supplement selling Article exists primarily to sell supplements — Biased source.
Raw food diets Any raw food recommendation — Unsafe for asplenia.
One-size-fits-all Ignores that conditions interact differently in each person.
Testimonial-only No citations, just personal stories presented as evidence.

Verdict Guide

Supported
Claims backed by peer-reviewed evidence, applicable to Shela's conditions
Mixed
Some valid points but incomplete evidence or not specific to her conditions
Unsupported
Claims lack evidence or contradict current medical consensus
Dangerous
Could actively harm Shela if followed (raw food, anti-med, etc.)
