CCA Daily Study Loop

Purpose: Give Shela a repeatable daily workflow that turns the official guide into usable exam judgment.

Daily Minimum Session: 30 Minutes

  1. Warm recall, 5 minutes
  • Ask 3 questions from yesterday or the weakest matrix rows.
  • Shela answers before seeing the explanation.
  • Mark misses in guide-mastery-matrix.md.
  1. One guide concept, 10 minutes
  • Pick exactly one row from guide-mastery-matrix.md.
  • Explain it using the Required Concept Card Format.
  • Tie it to one official scenario.
  1. Active drill, 10 minutes
  • Use one drill type: teach-back, spot-the-trap, scenario classification, or mock MCQ.
  • Grade against the official guide or guide-derived digest.
  • Require Shela to explain why the tempting wrong answer is wrong.
  1. Log and assign next action, 5 minutes
  • Update progress.md.
  • Update the relevant row in guide-mastery-matrix.md.
  • Name the next row or weak spot.

Deep Session: 45-60 Minutes

Use this when Shela has more energy.

  1. Warm recall, 5 minutes.
  2. Teach one new guide concept, 10 minutes.
  3. Drill the concept, 10 minutes.
  4. Scenario application, 10 minutes.
  5. Wrong-answer review, 10 minutes.
  6. Update logs and matrix, 5 minutes.

Daily Study Materials

Use these every day:

  • guide-mastery-matrix.md: what remains, what passed, what needs review.
  • CCA-vocabulary-dictionary.md: plain-English term review.
  • knowledge-base.md: guide-derived digest for fast lookup.
  • progress.md: current state, weak spots, and next session target.
  • readiness-gates.md: the criteria for exam readiness.

Shela's Responsibilities

Shela should:

  • answer before reading the explanation;
  • say "I don't know" quickly instead of guessing silently;
  • explain terms in her own words;
  • ask for a different analogy when the first one does not land;
  • rate confidence honestly from 1-5;
  • accept repetition on weak spots;
  • avoid using course completion as a substitute for exam practice;
  • keep daily review small enough to actually repeat.

Trainer Responsibilities

The trainer should:

  • keep the official guide as the governing source;
  • teach one concept at a time;
  • ask for active recall every session;
  • grade clearly and kindly;
  • update the matrix and progress log;
  • refuse to mark Passed without evidence;
  • keep Shela focused on scenario judgment, not trivia.
