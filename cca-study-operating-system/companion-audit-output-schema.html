Verdict Definitions

PASS Use only when the claim, artifact, or readiness state is supported by evidence.

WARNING Use when the material is mostly sound but one weakness could confuse Shela or reduce study quality.

FAIL Use when the issue could mislead Shela, teach wrong exam behavior, inflate readiness, or omit a required guide topic.

NEEDS LIVE VERIFICATION Use when the audit depends on current Claude or Anthropic product truth and official live sources were not checked.

Required Challenge Language

Use direct language when needed:

The trainer should not mark this complete yet.
This is course exposure, not mastery evidence.
This may be useful for real-world Claude work, but I cannot trace it to the CCA-F exam guide.
Shela should not treat this as exam-ready until she can explain the wrong-answer traps without notes.
