Genre-Specific Reading Tips
▶
Pentateuch (Genesis-Deuteronomy) ▶
Look for God's covenant promises and their progressive unfolding
Notice the pattern: God speaks, humans fail, God rescues
Pay attention to genealogies as the "thread of promise" leading to Christ
Leviticus: Don't get lost in ritual details; ask "what does this teach about holiness and access to God?"
Historical Narrative (Joshua-Esther) ▶
Focus on God's faithfulness despite human failure
Look for the cycle: obedience brings blessing, disobedience brings judgment, repentance brings restoration
Characters are not moral examples; they point to the need for a better King
Ask: "What does this reveal about God's character?"
Poetry & Wisdom (Job, Psalms, Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon) ▶
Read emotionally, not just intellectually. These are meant to be felt.
Psalms: Identify the type (lament, praise, thanksgiving, royal, wisdom)
Proverbs: These are general truths, not absolute promises
Job/Ecclesiastes: Embrace the tension. Not every question gets answered this side of eternity.
Prophets (Isaiah-Malachi) ▶
Always identify the historical context first (who is the prophet speaking to?)
Look for the pattern: judgment warning, call to repentance, promise of restoration
Messianic prophecies: Note how they point to Christ
Major themes: justice, faithfulness, God's sovereignty over nations
Gospels (Matthew-John) ▶
Jesus is the center. Every story, teaching, and miracle reveals who He is.
Notice what Jesus says about the Father
Pay attention to who He includes and who He confronts
Read the passion narrative slowly. This is where all of Scripture converges.
Acts ▶
The Spirit is the main character, not the apostles
Watch the gospel move from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth
Notice the pattern: proclamation, opposition, growth
Epistles (Romans-Jude) ▶
Identify the problem the letter addresses
Look for the indicative (what God has done) before the imperative (what we should do)
Trace the argument structure. Paul builds logical chains.
When IBCM reaches epistles: use Piper's "arcing" method to diagram argument flow
Revelation ▶
This is worship literature, not a puzzle to decode
Focus on the repeated theme: God wins, evil loses, Jesus reigns
Let the imagery create awe, not anxiety

Reading the Bible Supernaturally
John Piper (Crossway, 2017) - Complete Framework Analysis
Core Thesis
Bible reading is both a natural discipline (effort, method, consistency) and a supernatural act (requiring the Holy Spirit to open blind eyes). The goal is not information but seeing the glory of God in Scripture and being transformed by it.
"We are not mainly after knowledge about God. We are after God Himself."
Book Structure (3 Parts)
▶
Part 1: The Ultimate Goal (Ch 1-10)
WHY do we read the Bible?
Not for information or application (though both happen), but to see and savor the glory of God, to be satisfied in Him, and to be transformed from one degree of glory to another. Knowledge without affection is dead orthodoxy. We must not only see but savor, not only understand but delight.
Part 2: The Supernatural Act (Ch 11-13)
WHY does reading require a miracle?
"The natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God" (1 Cor 2:14). Without the Holy Spirit, Bible reading produces at best mere information. The Pharisees had perfect access, memorized vast portions, studied meticulously, and completely missed Jesus.
Part 3: The Natural Act of Reading Supernaturally (Ch 14-27)
HOW do we read practically?
Humility, prayer, faith, patience, and active questioning. The person who asks the most questions sees the most. Observation before interpretation before application. Every text reveals something about God; find it.
A.P.T.A.T. Pre-Reading Framework
▶
An alternative pre-reading approach. Write it on a bookmark:
Admit your inability (you cannot see God's glory on your own)
Pray for help (Psalm 119:18, 36)
Trust in a specific promise of God (He rewards those who seek Him)
Act by reading with aggressive attentiveness
Thank God for what He reveals

Brand Strategy ↔ Personal Development Strong
Pattern: The process of defining a brand's PURPOSE, VISION, MISSION, and VALUES is identical to personal goal-setting and value clarification.
Insight: You can apply the Brand Anatomy model to yourself to gain clarity on your own life's direction and how you present yourself to the world.
Applications: Create a "Personal Brand Anatomy" document. What is your purpose? Your vision for your life? Your mission for your career? Your non-negotiable values?
Content Marketing ↔ Teaching Strong
Pattern: The most effective content marketing is a form of teaching. The goal is to educate, inspire, and empower your audience, not just sell to them.
