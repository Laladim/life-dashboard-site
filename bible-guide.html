📖 How to Read Your Bible

You have two modes: a 2-minute quick read for consistency, and a deeper 15-30 minute study for when you have the time and energy. Both are valid. The goal is never zero.

⚡ Quick Read (2 minutes)

This is your daily minimum. On hard days, even one verse and one sentence counts.

  1. Check today's reading From the IBCM schedule in bible-reading-method.md, or ask Jarvis/Claude: today's reading
  2. Read the Bible Physical Bible or YouVersion app. Just read.
  3. Log it Message Jarvis or Claude Code:
    bible log: Joshua 11-15. Key verse: Josh 11:23. God completes what He starts.
The 2-minute rule: On hard days, just log the passage and one sentence. That counts. Never zero. Missing a day is not failure. Quitting is.
🧘 Deeper Study (15-30 minutes)

This is the full Piper method from Reading the Bible Supernaturally. Use it when you have the time and hunger for more.

BEFORE (2-5 min)

  1. Pray "Open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things out of your law." (Psalm 119:18)
  2. Humble yourself "I am a student, not a judge."
  3. Set context What book? What genre? Where in the story?
  4. Expect God to speak He has promised to speak. Come with faith.

DURING (main reading)

First pass: Read straight through. Get the flow.

Second pass: Piper's 5 questions:

  1. What does it say? (Observation)
  2. What does it mean? (Interpretation)
  3. What does it reveal about God? (Theology)
  4. What response does it call for? (Application)
  5. What is beautiful or surprising? (Worship)

Linger where the Spirit grabs you. Don't rush.

AFTER (2-5 min)

Journal using ACTS prayer:

  • Adoration: What can I praise God for from this passage?
  • Confession: What sin does this expose in me?
  • Thanksgiving: What am I grateful for?
  • Supplication: What should I ask God for?

Then log your key verse + one-sentence reflection. Carry one truth for the rest of the day.

The Navigators 12-month plan is designed for couples. Pace: 2-3 chapters/day across four books (currently Matthew, Acts, Psalms, Genesis).

Schedule: See navigators-month1.md for daily readings.
Discussion prompts:
  1. What stood out to you?
  2. What did this teach you about God?
  3. Is there something here we should apply this week?
  4. How does this connect to other things we've been reading?

If you miss a day, don't double up. Just pick up where you left off. When a passage sparks deep discussion, stay there. The schedule serves you, not the other way around.

📚 Genre-Specific Reading Tips

Different parts of the Bible use different literary forms. Knowing the genre changes how you read.

Pentateuch (Genesis-Deuteronomy)
Look for God's covenant promises and their progressive unfolding. Notice the pattern: God speaks, humans fail, God rescues.
Historical Narrative (Joshua-Esther)
Focus on God's faithfulness despite human failure. Characters are not moral examples; they point to the need for a better King.
Poetry & Wisdom (Job-Song of Solomon)
Read emotionally, not just intellectually. These are meant to be felt. Proverbs are general truths, not absolute promises.
Prophets (Isaiah-Malachi)
Always identify the historical context first. Look for: judgment warning, call to repentance, promise of restoration.
Gospels (Matthew-John)
Jesus is the center. Every story, teaching, and miracle reveals who He is. Read the passion narrative slowly.
Epistles (Romans-Jude)
Look for the indicative (what God has done) before the imperative (what we should do). Trace the argument structure.
Revelation
This is worship literature, not a puzzle to decode. Focus on: God wins, evil loses, Jesus reigns. Let the imagery create awe, not anxiety.

📝 Book Insights Storage

When you read a book (Piper, C.S. Lewis, or any other), capture your insights using this structure. Your first book-notes file (mere-christianity-notes.md in growth/insight-journal/desire/) serves as the template.

Template Structure
  1. Book metadata: Title, author, genre, why you're reading it
  2. Chapter-by-chapter notes: Key ideas, quotes, personal reflections
  3. Connections: Links to other books, Scripture, and life situations
  4. Teaching seeds: Ideas worth sharing or teaching later
  5. Overall takeaway: The one thing this book changed in your thinking
How to capture book insights: As you read, tell Claude Code or Jarvis: Book insight: [book title] - [your insight]. At the end of a book, ask Claude to help you create a full notes file. These become part of your permanent knowledge base in the insight journal.

🎯 10 Real Scenarios

Practical, step-by-step instructions for the most common situations you'll find yourself in.

1 Morning quick read
  1. Open your Bible or YouVersion to today's IBCM reading
  2. Read straight through (5-10 minutes)
  3. Pick one verse that stood out
  4. Message Jarvis: bible log: [passage]. Key verse: [ref]. [one sentence]
  5. Done. Move on with your day carrying that one truth.
2 Deeper study with heart preparation
  1. Open centering-page.md and read through the prayers and verses
  2. Pray Psalm 119:18: "Open my eyes..."
  3. Read today's passage twice (first for flow, second with Piper's 5 questions)
  4. Journal using ACTS: Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication
  5. Log: bible log: [passage]. Key verse: [ref]. [reflection]
  6. If a verse gripped you: Add verse: [reference]
3 You missed several days
  1. Don't panic. Don't try to "catch up" all at once.
  2. Ask Claude: catch up to see highlights of what you missed
  3. Read the catch-up summary in catch-up-genesis-to-joshua.md
  4. Pick up from today's reading. The summary gives you enough context to continue.
  5. Remember: consistency over perfection. Never zero.
"The devil's strategy is not to make you disbelieve the Bible but to make you too busy to read it carefully." John Piper
4 A verse hit you hard
  1. Stop. Don't rush past it. This is the Holy Spirit at work.
  2. Read it again slowly. What is it saying? Why is it gripping you?
  3. Pray about it. What is God showing you?
  4. Log the verse: Add verse: [reference] (goes to Verse Collection)
  5. If it triggered a deeper moment: Add moment: [describe what happened]
  6. Write it on a sticky note, set it as your phone wallpaper, or memorize it.
5 You have a theological question
  1. Say: Use skill: books-with-pastor-john-piper
  2. Ask your question (e.g., "Why does God allow suffering?" or "What does Piper say about anxiety?")
  3. Claude will pull relevant quotes from the 30-book library and guide you through Scripture
  4. The conversation gets logged to the Piper Wisdom tab in your Moments Database
  5. For deeper study, Claude can read the actual PDF of the most relevant book
6 Couple devotion with AJ
  1. Check today's Navigators reading in navigators-month1.md
  2. Read together (or individually, then discuss)
  3. Use the discussion prompts: What stood out? What does this teach about God?
  4. If something special happens in the conversation, capture it: Add moment: AJ and I discussed [topic] and realized...
  5. Don't pressure the schedule. If a passage sparks deep discussion, stay there.
7 Writing a prayer or poem
  1. Read practical-guidance-poetry.md for Piper's craft guidance
  2. Remember: purpose before technique. "The aim of Christian eloquence is not self-display but Christ-exaltation."
  3. Pay attention to: cadence, consonance, the right word, concreteness
  4. Write your prayer or poem
  5. Save it: Add prayer: [your text] (goes to Prayers & Poems tab)
"When we take care to create a beautiful way of speaking about something beautiful, the eloquence reflects and honors the beauty of the subject." John Piper
8 Content creation grounded in Scripture
  1. Pull relevant quotes from piper-quotes-index.md by topic
  2. Cross-reference with actual Scripture passages
  3. Use the Topical Cross-Reference section for specific life situations
  4. Quotes include book, chapter, and page references for attribution
  5. For Cobalt content: use the quotes to ground marketing in authentic conviction
9 Spiritual milestone reflection
  1. Ask Claude: Show moments or Review my moments this week
  2. Review your recent verses, moments, and prayers
  3. Look for patterns: What themes is God weaving through your life?
  4. Read a-passion-for-the-supremacy-of-god.md to reconnect with the "why"
  5. Capture any new insight: Add moment: Looking back, I see that God has been...
10 Starting insights from a new book
  1. Create a notes file: ask Claude to set up a book-notes template
  2. As you read, capture insights: Book insight: [title] - [insight]
  3. At chapter breaks, review and organize your notes
  4. When finished, ask Claude to summarize connections to other books and Scripture
  5. Add to the insight journal under the relevant tag page
